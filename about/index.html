About Me The man, the myth, the legend!

Connor J Douglas Programmer, Designer, Gamer, and more

I am Connor Douglas, an aspiring game developer from Honolulu, Hawai’i, currently studying Computer Science at the University of Utah. My involvement with the Robotics team in high school fostered an early interest in computer science, programming, and friendly technological competition. I’ve since pursued these interests and participate in game jams, hackathons, collaboative coding projects for my classes, and of course a healthy dose of multiplayer gaming in my free time.

When I am not immersed in the digital world, I enjoy basking on the beaches of Oahu, hiking the arid hills of Utah, biking the streets of Honolulu, and skiing the alpine basins of the Wasatch. I’m also an avid trombone player with eleven years of practice and performance in Hawai’i, London, New York, and the Berklee College of Music.

At the University of Utah's EAE program, I was in teams of 4 to 6 programmers, artists, designers, and producers. Parts Per Million, a climate focused hex tile game was made in one of these teams and was featured in the end of year EAE program party: EAE Days.

I love hearing about game development, computer science, or any thing else, so message me on twitter or shoot me an email.
