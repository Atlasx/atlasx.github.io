Parts Per Million

A climate focused hex map strategy game
with a stylized low poly aesthetic

Created by a team of six students at the University of Utah's EAE program, Parts Per Million is an alternative game focused on demonstrating the effects of climate change. Players fight food shortages, explosive population growth, and natural disasters in an attempt to sustain their island.

SystemsThe core components of the game

Weather A system designed to guide players

The weather system I built for Parts Per Million was intended to deliver swift and hard punishment on a player who was taking the wrong path through the game. With a series of categories for weather events enabled for scaling storms including dust storms, polluted rain, and hurricanes.

Hex Grid A procedural system for generating unique islands

The grid is the core component for Parts Per Million. With configureable biomes, cell types, blend parameters, and more the grid creates an entirely new island with different emergent gameplay in each playthrough. The heightmap is generated off of layered octaves of perlin noise multiplied with a radial gradient to get a coherent island shape. From there biomes are randomly planted throughout and grow until they cover the island completely.
